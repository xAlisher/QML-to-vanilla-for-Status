# Skill: Design Direction (from Brand Strategy PDF §21)

Source: `Brand Strategy_ Status .pdf` — always re-read before making design decisions.

## What we're doing
- Restrained, utility-first interface
- Monochrome or low-chroma palette
- High contrast, generous spacing
- Typographic hierarchy that reads like documentation, not advertising
- Honest materials: flat surfaces, minimal gradients, crisp dividers, clear affordances
- Simple, schematic icons; subtle, purposeful motion
- Editorial voice: plain language; terms and prices up front; clear, unfussy CTAs

## Typography as interface
- Modern grotesk UI (Inter) with monospace accents (Roboto Mono)
- Hierarchy via type scale; color used sparingly for status
- Monospace for labels, addresses, amounts — anywhere precision matters

## Two-lane cues
- Fast (L2) vs Private (Logos) differentiated by copy and micro-tokens
- Subtle shade shifts, not loud palettes

## Honest states
- Visible prices, terms, and implications (e.g., bridge disclosures)
- Empty states explain, not sell

## Risks and mitigations (from PDF)
- Perceived as plain/cold → warm neutrals, humane microcopy, friendly spacing, tasteful haptics
- Mistaken as dev-only → clear onboarding, obvious CTAs, example content, helpful tone
- Monotony → content variety (avatars, receipts, room badges) and editorial rhythm over decorative gradients

## Concept token palette targets
**Light:**
- Primary: desaturated warm blue (replacing #4360DF)
- Background: warm off-white (#FAFAF8 range)
- Text: high contrast black on warm neutrals
- Borders: subtler, warmer greys
- Nav sidebar: near-white (not blue-tinted #ECEFFB)

**Dark ("humane dark mode"):**
- Background: warm dark (#1A1A18 instead of cold #2C2C2C)
- Surfaces: warm layers (#262624)
- Text: off-white (#F0EEEB) instead of pure white
- Primary accent: desaturated warm blue
- Subtle surface hierarchy via warm neutral steps

## One-line rationale (from PDF)
> We're choosing a calm, utilitarian design to communicate privacy, control, and seriousness at a glance — trading shiny persuasion for trustworthy, legible tools people use every day.
